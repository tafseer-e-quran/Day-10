ADDING PRESSURE IN WORDS

FOR EXAMPLE
Meaning
یَضۡرِبُ He beats (Present) / He will beat (Future)

If we add لَا in the starting than it becomes the opposite meaning:


FOR EXAMPLE
Meaning
لَا یَضۡرِبُ He does not beats (Present) / He will not beat (Future)

If we add a sakin in the end than it becomes an order:


FOR EXAMPLE
Meaning
لَا یَضۡرِبۡ He should not beat. [This is an Order]
لَا تَضۡرِبۡ You should not beat. [This is an Order]. You can add other pronouns as well.

What if we want to order the person to beat. For that we just add لَ in the starting:

FOR EXAMPLE
Meaning
یَضۡرِبُ He beats (Present) / He will beat (Future)
لَیَضۡرِبۡ He should beat. [This is an Order]
لَیَضۡرِبَا They [both] should beat. [This is an Order]
لَتَضۡرِبۡ You should beat. [This is an Order]. You can use other pronouns as well.

If you get confused than don't be as when we will start the Quran you will easily understand it. This is something that we wanted you to keep in mind.


In Quran, another word is also used to say "No" that is لَمۡ. It is used for past. Even if you use it with a present tense word, the sentence becomes past. For example :

FOR EXAMPLE
Meaning
یَضۡرِبُ He beats (Present) / He will beat (Future)
لَمۡ یَضۡرِبۡ He did not beat. [Note: This sentence is in past]
لَمۡ یَضۡرِبَا They [both] did not beat. [Note: This sentence is in past]
لَمۡ تَضۡرِبۡ You did not beat. [Note: This sentence is in past]. You can use other pronouns as well.

In Quran, another word is also used to say "No" that is لَنۡ. It is used for future. Even if you use it with a present tense word, the sentence becomes future. It is also adds pressure in the sentence. For example :

FOR EXAMPLE
Meaning
یَضۡرِبُ He beats (Present) / He will beat (Future)
لَنۡ یَّضۡرِبَ He will definitely not beat. [Note: This sentence is in future]
لَنۡ یَّضۡرِبَا They [both] will definitely not beat. [Note: This sentence is in future]
لَنۡ تَّضۡرِبَ You will definitely not beat. [Note: This sentence is in future]. You can use other pronouns as well.

Note: Add a Tajdeed on the first letter of the word.

SEEGAH E MUBALGA
[صیغہ مبالغہ]

words like غَافِرۡ are also written in the shape of غَفَّارۡ to add pressure in it.

FOR EXAMPLE
Meaning
غَافِرۡ forgiving
غَفَّارۡ too much forgiving
سَاتِرۡ One who covers/hides [e.g. sins]
سَتَّارۡ One who covers too much
وَاھِبۡ Gifter / One who gifts
وَھَّابۡ Too much gifter / one who gifts too much
ظَالِمۡ Oppressor / unjust
ظَلَّامۡ Too much oppressor / too much unjust

words like غَافِرۡ are also written in the shape of غَفُورۡ to add pressure in it.

FOR EXAMPLE
Meaning
غَافِرۡ forgiving
غَفُورۡ too much forgiving
صَابِرۡ Patient / One with patience
صَبُورۡ One with too much patience
وَادِدۡ Loving / One who is loving
وَدُودۡ Too much Loving
وَالِدۡ Child bearer
وَلُودۡ Too much child bearer
شَاكِرۡ Thankful / One who is thankful. It also means One who Values
شَكُورۡ To much thankful. One who too much Values

Some of these words are also written in the shape of فَعۡلَانۡ.

FOR EXAMPLE
Meaning
رَاحِمۡ Merciful
رَحِیمۡ One whose attribute / quality is Merciful. Meaning one who is merciful everytime [i.e. Allah [S.W.T]]
رَحۡمٰنۡ One who is Merciful. [It is in the shape of فَعۡلَانۡ]

These three words are used differently in the Quran but we won't go that much deep so you don't get confused. You just have to remember, than whenever these words come, you should there meanings.


Note : In "Seegah e Mubalga", there are no genders. Same word is used for both genders. For example غَفُورۡ or غَفَّارۡ is used for both male and female.
